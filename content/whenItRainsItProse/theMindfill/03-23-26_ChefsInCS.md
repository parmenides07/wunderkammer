### Chef Analogy
- Lets set up the metaphor of two chefs. I'm sure you're very familiar wtih all those cooking videos where there is the level 1 chef, the level 2 chef, and the level 3 chef. 

1. So in level 1, the chef is a complete amateur. They have very little experience with cooking and so they follow a recipe. They typically follow recipes to a tee but depending on the recipes, the final food in the end is not always perfect. However, when they do use the same recipe, they are able to get the same food produced over and over. 
2. In this level, you have a home cook. A chef that sometimes needs to refer to the recipe but can kinda fill in the blanks. They know what tastes good and how to enhance what's there, but they're still basically working off of the recipe. Maybe they'd use thyme instead of dill because it tastes better with the acidic flavors or the dish or maybe they'd put a little bit extra tomato paste. Here, they get a pretty different result every time the recipe is used. 
3. In this level, you have a master chef. This is one that cooks at restaurants. They don't need recipes. They do everything completely intuitively and know exactly how long to cook something for and what to put in it. They know that reducing the tomatoes for a minute extra but no more would bring out the acidic flavor without turning them bitter. They know the ins and outs of everything and have internalized the ingredients so much so that they can just make up recipes on the go. They do things completely intuitively.

### Explanation
I'm not sure if I made it subtle enough or clear enough but this can be likened to computers. 

1. For most of the time that computer science has existed, we've tried really nailing computers being complete amateurs. These computers know how to follow these recipes to a tee. Sometimes the recipe dosen't function properly but that's the fault of the recipe writer aka, the programmer.
2. Next level is where I'd say we are currently at. We have implimented LLMs that are able to add on to the recipes we write, not fully intuitive yet but they are able to make the recipe a lot better and improvize. 
3. What sits in the future, however, is computer science developing the technology to achieve the master chef status. Here, the computer would be able to come up with stuff on its own and be different every time, using tons and tons of internalized knowledge, thinking and making decisions on the go. This is AGI (or maybe if we never reach AGI, a very advanced form of what we are currently working towards).

- Now, an interesting thing to note is that currently, many CS majors work on writing those recipes. As in, they write the code that drives the computer. As we can see, this past year or two, the number of recipe-writers needed has decreased as we have progressed into the 2nd stage of the technology. So this begs the question, what role will CS and CS graduates play when recipes are no longer needed? What qualifications will they have over others to tell the master chef what to cook?
