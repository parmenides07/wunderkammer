Before anything let me preface a few things: 

# Pure Asethetics
I do not believe art has has to have meaning. What I derive pleasure from in art is the asethetics. I want to try to refine the more technical aspects of art. This is the most interesting part of the medium. 
# Visual Art and Other Mediums
Visual art is unique from other mediums in the potential for creation it has. Since it's a medium that plays on the visual senses, it has vastly more possibilities than other mediums (ie. music). Don't believe me? Well just think about it. The last sense you'd likely forgo is sight. It's by far our most important, thus making the medium of art that pertains to it that much more significant. Due to this, art's skill ceiling is immense. 
# Hyperrealism is Lame (Mostly)
A common idea that starts to form when I explain my goals in art is that I must love hyperrealism. Wrong. I am staunchly against most forms of hyperrealism. The nature of art as a whole is making possible and creating the impossible. Why create that which can be so easily seen? To embark on the journey of improving one's technical skills, you must chart a course for something beyond, realism. Post-realism [[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UozmOJajIgo]]. Here, the true mastery of skills like perspective and color and anatomy can shine, free from the shackles of reality and all its constraints. You might bring up the point of old masters and their pursuit of realism. Even they observed some principles of post-realism and making something look more real than reality. Hyperrealism does away with even this approach.
